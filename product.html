Product Information

ROMetrics provides interactive tools for learning, practicing, and applying movement assessment through structured modules and simulations. The platform is organized into five functional areas, each with a distinct purpose and workflow.

1. Goniometry Trainer

The Goniometry Trainer is used to practice joint angle measurement using a virtual goniometer on an anatomically accurate 3D model. This module is guided and instructional; it is where users learn how to measure.

How it is used

  • Select a model and body position (standing, sitting, supine, prone).
  • Choose a body region and joint action.
  • Place the goniometer by selecting the Fulcrum, Stationary arm, and Moving arm.
  • Press Start ROM to move the joint through its range while the goniometer tracks the angle.
  • Read and record the measured value.

What this teaches

  • Proper goniometer placement and joint-specific measurement technique.
  • Plane and axis awareness.
  • How positioning affects measurements.

2. Manual Muscle Testing (MMT)

The MMT Trainer focuses on muscle testing concepts and grading logic, rather than simple memorization.

How it is used

  • Select an MMT test from the muscle list and review test position and movement direction.
  • Choose the appropriate grade range (Grades 0–2 or 3–5).
  • Apply resistance using the interactive control.
  • Observe hold time, fatigue, and break response to assign a grade.

What this teaches

  • Muscle function, action, and proper test positioning.
  • Grading logic and exceptions.
  • The mechanical reasoning behind where resistance is applied.

3. Motion Lab

The Motion Lab allows users to freely explore human movement without measurement or grading. This lab is exploratory, not evaluative.

How it is used

  • Select a body region and move joints manually or through predefined actions.
  • Observe segment motion, coordination, and sequencing.
  • Adjust posture, model orientation, and view angle.

What this teaches

  • How joints move in isolation and combination.
  • Segment relationships and movement direction.
  • Foundational motion understanding before formal testing.

4. Simulated Evaluations

Simulated Evaluations are case-based assessments that mirror real evaluation flow. This is where training becomes application.

How they work

  • Users are provided with case context, clues, and patient history.
  • The user must decide what to observe, what to measure, and which tools to use.
  • Cases include range-of-motion limitations, strength deficits, and postural impairments.

Purpose

  • Apply learned skills without guidance.
  • Practice clinical decision-making.
  • Prepare for practical exams and real-world assessments.

5. Forensic Motion Lab

The Forensic Motion Lab is used to analyze movement outcomes—not to measure for treatment—answering the question: “Why did this happen?”

What it focuses on

  • Balance and stability.
  • Relationship between Center of Mass and Base of Support.
  • Compensatory strategies, joint sequencing, and posture.

How it is used

  • Observe a movement or task outcome in a specific forensic module.
  • Adjust posture, joint motion, or strategy to analyze stability.
  • Identify mechanical reasons for success or failure.
