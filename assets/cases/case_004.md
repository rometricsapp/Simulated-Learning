# Case 4: Shoulder  Impairment

## Patient Profile
- Name: Daniel M.
- Age: 34
- Occupation: Software engineer

---

## Chief Complaint
“My right shoulder feels frozen. I can’t reach overhead or behind my back, and it hurts a lot at night.”

---

## History of Present Illness
- Onset **gradual**, over the last **4–5 months**
- No specific injury or trauma recalled
- Progressive loss of motion in the **right shoulder**
- Pain described as deep, aching, and constant (5–7/10)
- **Night pain** present; frequently wakes from sleep when lying on right side
- Reports increasing difficulty with:
  - Reaching overhead
  - Putting on a jacket
  - Reaching behind back to tuck in shirt
- Symptoms **worsen with inactivity**
- Mild temporary relief with NSAIDs

### Relevant Medical History
- Type II Diabetes Mellitus (diagnosed 6 years ago)
- Reports recent **unintentional weight loss** and persistent fatigue
- No prior shoulder pathology
- Denies numbness, tingling, or neck pain

---

## Objective Examination

### Shoulder ROM (Right)

| Motion               | Normal | Actual                | Notes                                      |
| -------------------- | ------ | --------------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| External Rotation    | 90°    | **20°** (painful)     | **Most restricted**                        |
| Abduction            | 180°   | **70°** (painful)     | Firm, capsular end-feel                    |
| Internal Rotation    | 70°    | **30°** (painful)     | Limited, stiff                             |
| Flexion              | 180°   | 110°                  | Limited by pain and stiffness              |
| Extension            | 60°    | 40°                   | Mild restriction                           |

**Pattern observed:**
- **External Rotation ↓↓↓ > Abduction ↓↓ > Internal Rotation ↓**
- Classic **glenohumeral capsular pattern**

**Visible impairment:**
- Shoulder hiking during attempted abduction
- Guarded movement with facial grimacing
- Avoids end ranges

### Sensation / Neuro Screen

- Reflexes normal

## Case Clues
- Progressive **loss of shoulder ROM without trauma**
- **Capsular pattern** of restriction (ER > ABD > IR)
- Significant **night pain**
- History of **diabetes**
- Systemic symptoms (fatigue, weight loss) requiring consideration
- No neurologic involvement
